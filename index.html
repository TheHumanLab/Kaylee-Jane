Selected Diagnostics — THL Framework

“Feeling Stuck”
High-Capability Individual

Symptoms: Strong starts followed by hesitation, reset, and drop-off. Perceived as lack of discipline.

Core Structural Fault: Multiple systems active with no lead function. No system holds authority over decision-making.

01 DIAGNOSE

Progress System → initiates
Uncertainty System → interrupts
Identity System → destabilises direction

02 DEFINE

The Stuck Cycle: Strong Start → Hesitation → Drop-off → Reset. No lead system designated.

03 REDESIGN

Establish Decision System as lead.
Remove uncertainty from execution.
Delay identity evaluation until after action.

04 OUTCOME

Consistent follow-through. Reduced internal friction.
Stable direction over time.
System now has a lead function. Direction holds. Execution flows.

Leadership Authority
COO, Large Organisation

Symptoms: Decisions delayed, softened, or revisited. Authority inconsistent across teams.

Core Structural Fault: Authority is being negotiated, not exercised. Competing systems with no authority structure leading decisions.

01 DIAGNOSE

Competence System → over-analysis
Social Risk System → deference to alignment
Role System → unstable authority

02 DEFINE

Three active systems competing. No lead system designated. Authority negotiated per-decision.

03 REDESIGN

Establish Authority as lead system.
Reassign analysis + alignment to advisory roles.
Lock decisions once made.

04 OUTCOME

Clear direction. Stable authority.
Execution without re-negotiation.
Authority exercised. Decisions held. Execution flows.

Systems Glossary

Key
Distinctions

LEAD SYSTEM

The system with authority to execute. When no lead is designated, competing systems negotiate every decision, creating friction and delay.

STRUCTURAL FAULT

The architectural defect producing the symptom. Behavior is output; structure is cause. Diagnosis targets the fault, not the behavior.

DECISION ARCHITECTURE

The explicit rules, triggers, and feedback loops that produce choices. Most are invisible until mapped. Once visible, they can be redesigned.

BEHAVIORAL CIRCUIT

The stimulus → rule → output chain behind recurring patterns. Intervention at the circuit level breaks loops permanently.
